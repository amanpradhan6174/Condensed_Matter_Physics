Q3(b) ANALYSIS: EFFECT OF NEXT-NEAREST-NEIGHBOUR HOPPING


Model Description:
- Square lattice with nearest and next-nearest neighbour hopping
- Nearest-neighbour hopping: t = -2.00 eV
- Next-nearest neighbour hopping: t'' = ±1.0 eV

Band Structure:
- Energy range: [-12.0000, 4.0000] eV
- Dispersion: E(k) = -2t (cos kx + cos ky) - 4t'' cos kx cos ky

Numerical Results:
- Half-filling Fermi energy (t'' = -1.0 eV): Ef ≈ -1.9653 eV
- Half-filling Fermi energy (t'' = +1.0 eV): Ef ≈ 1.9066 eV

Comparison with Q3(a):
- Q3(a) had particle-hole symmetry
- Inclusion of t'' breaks this symmetry
- Fermi level shifts away from band center

Fermi Surface Evolution:
- t'' > 0:
  * Fermi surface becomes more rounded
  * Band curvature increases

- t'' < 0:
  * Fermi surface becomes more square-like
  * Strong anisotropy appears

Physical Interpretation:
- Next-nearest hopping introduces diagonal electronic pathways
- This modifies band curvature and density of states
- Breaks particle-hole symmetry of the lattice

Topological Insight:
- Change in Fermi surface shape indicates possible Lifshitz transition
- Electronic properties strongly depend on sign of t''

Conclusion:
- Inclusion of t'' significantly alters electronic structure
- Sign of t'' controls Fermi surface geometry
- System shows richer physics compared to nearest-neighbour model
