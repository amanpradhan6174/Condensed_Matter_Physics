Q3(a) ANALYSIS: SQUARE LATTICE (NEAREST-NEIGHBOUR)


Model Description:
- Square lattice
- Nearest-neighbour hopping only
- Hopping parameter: t = -2.00 eV

Band Structure:
- Energy range: [-8.0000, 7.9996] eV
- Dispersion: E(k) = -2t (cos kx + cos ky)

Numerical Results:
- Fermi energy at half filling (n = 1.0): Ef ≈ -0.0197 eV

Filling Dependence:
- Filling n represents electrons per unit cell (max = 2 due to spin)
- Occupied fraction of states = n/2

Fermi Surface Evolution:
- At low filling (n ≈ 0.5): small electron pockets
- At intermediate filling: contour expands across Brillouin zone
- At half filling (n = 1): symmetric Fermi surface
- Near full filling (n → 2): contour shrinks toward band edge

Physical Interpretation:
- The system exhibits particle-hole symmetry
- Fermi energy at half filling lies near band center
- No distortion or asymmetry without next-nearest hopping

Conclusion:
- Square lattice with nearest-neighbour hopping shows symmetric band structure
- Fermi surface evolves smoothly with filling
- Serves as reference for comparison with Q3(b)
