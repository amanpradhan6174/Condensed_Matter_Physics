Q2 ANALYSIS: EFFECT OF DIAGONAL HOPPING


Model Description:
- Base system: Two-leg ladder
- Nearest-neighbour hopping: t = 1.00
- Inter-chain hopping: t'
- Diagonal hopping: t'' = 0.20

Numerical Results:
Q2(a): Single diagonal hopping
- Minimum gap: -3.6000
- Maximum gap: 4.0000
- Critical point: t' ≈ 2.0000

Q2(b): Crossed diagonal hopping
- Minimum gap: -3.2000
- Maximum gap: 4.0000
- Critical point: t' ≈ 2.0000

Comparison with Q1:
- In Q1, transition occurs at t' ≈ 2t
- Addition of t'' shifts the transition point
- The shift depends on geometry of diagonal hopping

Physical Interpretation:
- Q2(a):
  * Off-diagonal term includes complex phase (e^ik)
  * Breaks inversion symmetry
  * Leads to asymmetric band dispersion

- Q2(b):
  * Off-diagonal term ~ cos(k)
  * Preserves symmetry
  * Modifies bandwidth without phase asymmetry

Key Insight:
- Diagonal hopping alters electronic coupling pathways
- Geometry of hopping strongly influences band structure
- Both cases still show a single metal–insulator transition

Conclusion:
- Presence of t'' shifts and modifies the transition
- Q2(a) and Q2(b) behave differently due to symmetry differences
