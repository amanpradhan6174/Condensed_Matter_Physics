Q1 ANALYSIS: TWO-LEG LADDER MODEL
----------------------------------

Model Description:
- System: Two-leg ladder
- Nearest-neighbour hopping along chain: t = 1.00
- Inter-chain hopping: t'

Numerical Results:
- Minimum gap observed: -4.0000
- Maximum gap observed: 4.0000
- Estimated critical point: t' ≈ 2.0000

Band Structure Interpretation:
- Energy dispersion: E(k) = -2t cos(k) ± t'
- Increasing t' splits bonding and antibonding bands

Phase Behaviour:
- For t' < 2.00: bands overlap → METALLIC phase
- At t' ≈ 2.00: gap closes → CRITICAL point
- For t' > 2.00: finite gap → INSULATING phase

Physical Insight:
- Inter-chain coupling enhances localization
- The transition occurs when t' ≈ 2t
- No re-entrant phase is observed (single transition)

Conclusion:
- The ladder system exhibits a single metal–insulator transition
- Controlled entirely by inter-chain hopping strength
